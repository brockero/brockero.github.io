At-Risk Youth Design Project

CURRENT - Project utilizing design thinking centered around at-risk youth, August-December 2016

I am currently wrapping up a project focused on teaching the design thinking method, with a focus on at-risk youth. Working on the project in a group of five, we've worked through five different phases as we prepare for our final presentation.

Initially, we started with the broad topic of "at-risk youth." After conducting research and synthesis, we shifted our focus to bullying. This further shifted to a specific focus on inclusivity and appreciating diversity in elementary schools.

Working through our ideas, we came up with three different teaching methods: a series of books, a computer game, and a classroom activity program. Together with our instructor, we eventually decided to develop it as one package. This program is known as The IDEAL: Inclusive Diversity Engagement Activity Learning.

For the purposes of our class, we developed a prototype of the game, a sample book storyboard and lesson plan, and a group of classroom activities we call "Say Something." Together we created the ideas behind a new approach to the anti-bullying program while learning the design thinking process. In the end, we will present our findings to our class as well as a staff member from the Michigan Department of Education.
